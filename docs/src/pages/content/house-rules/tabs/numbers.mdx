#### Comma (,)

In four or more digits long numbers, use commas to set off groups of three digits, counting leftward from the decimal point.

**Note:** Do not use a comma to the right of decimal point.

#### Decimal (.)

A decimal separator (.) is used to separate the integer and the fractional part of a number written in decimal form.

**Note:** For long decimal numbers, use comma separators to the left of the decimal point not otherwise.

#### Percent (%)

Spell out "percent" with numerals unless there are either space constraints or the content is data-intensive (such as tables, charts and dashboards).

**Note:** If the percentage starts the sentence, then spell out both the number and the word percent.

#### Units (mg/dl, lbs, $, etc.)

Insert a space between the numeral and the unit.<br/>
Put a dollar sign '$' before the amount when talking about US currency. Use decimal and number of cents if more than 0. Follow the same with other currencies.
